The halo was first considered in 2009 after the tragic death of Henry Surtees in Formula 2. Surtees’ accident was a result of a tire coming off another car and hitting Surtees in the head causing fatal injuries to his head. This was a wakeup call to the FIA(Federation Internationale de l’Automobile), which is the governing body of the Formula series. The halo was chosen over the aeroscreen because of the strength of the titanium. Teams tested the halo by throwing wheels at it to see how much energy could be absorbed.
